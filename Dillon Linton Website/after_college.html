After College Plan

After I graduate in December, I have a well-planned after-college strategy to kickstart my career in information technology. My first step is to obtain the CompTIA Trifecta certification, which includes A+, Network+, and Security+. These certifications will lay a strong foundation in IT fundamentals and security, making me a competitive candidate for entry-level IT positions.

While pursuing my certifications, I'll be actively trying to break into the IT field. I'm excited to gain hands-on experience and put my knowledge to the test in real-world scenarios. Whether it's through internships, part-time roles, or volunteer opportunities, I'm determined to make the most of every chance to learn and grow in the industry.

Simultaneously, I'm planning to apply for Western Governors University's cybersecurity master's program. This advanced degree will allow me to specialize in a field that I'm passionate about and one that holds immense significance in today's digital landscape. I'm particularly drawn to WGU's program because of its flexibility, which means I can balance my studies with my professional commitments.

As I navigate this exciting phase in my life, I know the key to success lies in staying curious, adaptable, and proactive. The IT industry is constantly evolving, and I'm committed to staying up-to-date with the latest trends and technologies. Armed with a combination of certifications, practical experience, and a cybersecurity master's degree, I'm confident in my ability to excel in the IT field and contribute significantly to the world of cybersecurity. Here's to a bright and fulfilling future in information technology!
